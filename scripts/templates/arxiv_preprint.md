guide Abstract: 6-8 sentences; scope, findings, and evidence sources.
guide Background: define key terms and prior work.
guide Methods: note data sources, pipelines, and tools.
guide Results: highlight the main evidence with caveats.
guide Discussion: interpret results; compare alternatives.
guide Conclusion: summarize takeaways and next steps.
guide Risks & Gaps: highlight missing evidence, constraints, unknowns, and validation needs. If not applicable, state Not applicable and give a brief reason; scale depth to evidence strength and context.
guide Critics: short headline + brief paragraphs + bullets; include contrarian views. If not applicable, state Not applicable and give a brief reason; scale depth to evidence strength and context.
writer_guidance: Be explicit about assumptions and data limits.
writer_guidance: Maintain reproducibility notes where applicable.
